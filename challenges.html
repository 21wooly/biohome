Challenge Factors of BioHousing

The construction and implentation of a BioHouse in a community calls for numerous obstacles to be passed before it's start. Listed below are many  possible   challenges that can be faced with different orginizations or systems because of your choosing to 'go biofriendly'.                 
Enviorenmental Protection Agency (EPA)

The Environmental Protection Agency do support and endorse those who chose to go green in their housing. In special circumstances they provide funding and grants. However, The regulations they set don’t always show appreciation to each state equally. The EPA is limited in their own funding and how they can help.

Zoning

Depending on the scope of a project, the zoning/planning board may require a specially advertised public hearing on a project for either the initial presentation or subsequent hearings. Zoning hearings often mandate that any zoning or use change will only be authorized if the subject parcel is brought into full compliance with existing building code statutes. Current criteria must also be met by certain site improvements as well, including: Landscaping, Water retention, and Parking requirements.

Permits

If the contractor does not seek to acquire a permit for the work, the owner may later experience problems that the contractor caused either through cutting corners or a lack of knowledge in how to replace or fix certain items. Without the permit, the person may face serious consequences. A lawyer may halt the problem until the owner is able to acquire the necessary permit. However, if the owner did follow the correct steps, the legal professional may need to explain this to the local authorities. The most significant problem of not acquiring the permit is a city inspector. No matter who contacts him or her, the inspector may shut down the entire operation.  

Home Owner's Assosciations 

When you purchase a home or plot of land in a neighborhood with a Homeowners Association (HOA) , you must agree to the terms and conditions they set for you. Basically, they have some authority over your property. Any change you plan entails their juridstriction. In a sense, they mandate the aesthetic of your house. Guidelines can limit the building process and/or projects temporarily or even completely.  Guidelines are also backed by laws, so interfering with them can get you into legal trouble.

Technological Appliances

Biofriendly housing means low energy consumption and less carbon emissions. Every house has electrical appliances that depends on circuit breakers installed in the ground which costs a lot and needs a lot to be powered. Phones, fridges, computers, etc are appliances that are heavily electrically dependant. Switching over to other forms of power could mean they aren’t powered all the time when they need to be.

Home Insurance

Many home insurance companies are endorsing the “green” movement of home building. Companies like Travelers, and Farmers are noted for this. Eco-policies and opportunities are becoming more mainstream and pushed for in insurance strategy. Challenges could be having higher quotes since your house is optimized with several taboo features or new “green” components that haven’t been used for long enough. Sadly, some companies won’t provide quotes given that your house is biofriendly.
